Managing Your Daily Routine

Establishing structure and balance as you prepare for your return to the workforce.

Creating an Effective Return-to-Work Routine

  • Morning Preparation

    Start your day with purpose by waking up at a consistent time, setting intentions, and planning your day. Try to wake up 30-60 minutes before your household to focus on yourself first.

  • Dedicated Skill Development Time

    Reserve 1-2 hours daily for upskilling activities such as online courses, reading industry publications, or practicing technical skills. Consistency is more important than duration.

  • Job Search Block

    Allocate specific time (30-60 minutes daily) for job search activities including researching companies, tailoring your resume, and applying to positions. This prevents job searching from consuming your entire day.

  • Networking Time

    Schedule 15-30 minutes daily to maintain professional connections through LinkedIn engagement, following up with contacts, or reaching out to potential mentors.

  • Family/Personal Time Blocks

    Deliberately schedule quality time with family and for self-care to maintain balance and prevent burnout. Create clear boundaries between career preparation and personal time.

  • Weekly Review and Planning

    End each week with a 30-minute review of your progress and plan for the upcoming week. Celebrate small wins and adjust as needed.

Time Blocking Technique

Rather than multitasking, dedicate focused blocks of time to specific tasks. Use the Pomodoro technique (25 minutes of focused work followed by a 5-minute break) to maintain productivity.

Transition Preparation

If you've been out of the workforce for a while, gradually adjust your schedule to match typical work hours. This helps ease the transition when you start your new role.

Childcare Planning

Begin researching and testing childcare arrangements before you actually need them. This allows you to resolve issues and helps children adjust gradually to new routines.

Personal Branding & Online Presence

Resources to help you build a compelling professional presence online and offline.

Creating an Effective LinkedIn Profile

  • Professional Photo

    Upload a clear, professional headshot where you're smiling and dressed appropriately for your industry. This increases profile views by up to 14 times.

  • Compelling Headline

    Go beyond just a job title. Include keywords and highlight your unique value proposition. For example: "Marketing Professional | Brand Strategist | Helping Companies Connect with Their Audience"

  • Strategic Summary

    Write in first person and tell your career story, including your break. Highlight your passion, key skills, and what you're looking for now. Keep it to 3-5 short paragraphs.

  • Experience Section

    List your previous roles with accomplishments, not just responsibilities. For your career gap, consider adding entries for relevant volunteer work, freelance projects, or education during this time.

  • Skills & Endorsements

    Add 5-10 key skills relevant to your target roles. Ask former colleagues to endorse you and consider taking LinkedIn's skill assessments to earn badges.

  • Regular Activity

    Post or share content at least weekly. Comment thoughtfully on industry posts. This keeps you visible in your network's feed and demonstrates your engagement in your field.
